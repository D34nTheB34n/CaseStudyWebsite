Executive Summary

This case study examines the critical issue of limited sustainable internet access in Sub-Saharan Africa, focusing on Nigeria, the Central African Republic, and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. These regions face significant barriers that hinder economic growth and educational opportunities. Implementing mesh networks can provide scalable and sustainable solutions, while considering stakeholders in alignment with UN Sustainable Development Goals.

Introduction

Background Information

Sub-Saharan Africa struggles with low internet access rates, with only about 28% penetration as of 2021, compared to 60% globally. Historical, political, and geographical factors contribute to this digital gap, especially in rural communities.

Importance of the Case Study

Internet access is essential for education and economic growth. The COVID-19 pandemic has intensified the need for digital connectivity, making solutions critical for recovery and growth.

Objectives of the Study

  • Understand the factors influencing internet accessibility.
  • Advocate for sustainable solutions.
  • Raise awareness of internet access's impact on economic growth.
  • Develop actionable plans for infrastructure improvement.

Literature Review

Doughnut Economics Overview

Doughnut Economics, created by Kate Raworth, provides a model for social and environmental balance. It emphasizes meeting human needs without surpassing ecological limits, integrating economic and environmental goals.

Systems Thinking Perspective

Systems Thinking allows us to address the multifaceted challenges of internet access holistically, recognizing the interconnectedness of historical, geographical, and economic factors.

Methodology

This study employs a mixed-methods approach:

  • Qualitative Data Collection: Interviews and focus groups with stakeholders.
  • Quantitative Data Collection: Surveys and secondary data analysis from reputable organizations.

Case Presentation

This case study examines three regions:
Lagos (Nigeria): A financial hub with significant disparities in internet access.
Bangui (Central African Republic): A capital with less than 4% internet penetration.
Kinshasa (Democratic Republic of the Congo): A densely populated city facing infrastructural challenges.

Analysis

Several key factors impact internet access:

  • Political Instability: Affects consistency in telecommunications policies.
  • Geographical Barriers: Difficult terrains increase infrastructure costs.
  • Economic Limitations: Low income levels restrict access to affordable services.

Findings

Key insights indicate:

  1. Access to the internet enhances educational opportunities.
  2. Job creation arises from improved digital infrastructure.
  3. Increased connectivity can lead to higher GDP rates in local economies.

Recommendations

To address the challenges identified, we propose:

  • Implement Mesh Networking Solutions for sustainable internet.
  • Invest in Renewable Energy Infrastructure for reliable access.
  • Encourage Polycentric Approaches for collaborative stakeholder engagement.

Conclusion

This case study emphasizes that sustainable internet access is crucial for social and economic development. By leveraging innovative technologies and frameworks like Doughnut Economics and Systems Thinking, we can foster collaboration toward a brighter future for underserved communities.
